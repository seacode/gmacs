Jan 3, 2015. Working with John Levitt on analytical solution instead of the numerical approach. Think we have a soln.

Notation:
\(n\) = vector of newshell crabs
\(o\) = vector of oldshell crabs
\(P\) = diagonal matrix of molting probabilities by size
\(S\) = diagonal matrix of survival rates by size
\(A\) = Size transition matrix
\(r\) = vector of new recruits (newshell)
\(I\) = identity matrix.

The following equations represent the dynamics of newshell n and oldshell crabs.

\begin{align*} n &= nSPA + oSPA + r \\ o &= oS(I-P) + nS(I-P) \end{align*}

Objective is to solve the above equations for \(n\) and \(o\) repsectively. First, lets solve the second equation for \(o\):

\begin{align*} o &= n(I-P)S[I-(I-P)S]^{-1} \end{align*}

next substitute the above expression into first equation above and solve for \(n\)

\begin{align*} n &= nPSA + n(I-P)S[I-(I-P)S]^{-1}PSA + r \\ \mbox{let} \quad \beta& = [I-(I-P)S]^{-1}, \\ r &= n - nPSA - n(I-P)S \beta PSA \\ r &= n(I - PSA - (I-P)S \beta PSA) \\ \mbox{let} \quad C& = (I - PSA - (I-P)S \beta PSA), \\ n &= (C)^{-1} (r) \end{align*}

Note that \(C\) must be invertable to solve for the equilibrium solution for \(n\). So the diagonal elements of \(P\) and \(S\) must be positive non-zero numbers.
